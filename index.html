MAKING THE ROCKET FUEL + BODY

Nov 8, 2018

/Science Rockets

On the 23rd of October, we practiced rolling the fuel capsules with normal white paper and then did three good copies using pink card.

On the 24th of October, we tested our rocket fuel ratios. The rocket fuel was made up of icing sugar and Potassium Nitrate. We chose the ratio 65% icing sugar 35% Potassium Nitrate. We tested 50%-50%, 60%-40% and 80%-20%. First, we put the icing sugar on a tin lid and weighed how much we needed. Then we weighed the Potassium Nitrate. We lit them on fire to see which one had the biggest bang and was on fire for the longest.

On the 25th of October, we made the right amount and put it in the three fuel capsules we the plugged the top with epoxy compound, this stopped the Potassium Nitrate from falling out.

On the 31st of October, we launched a practice rocket and ours went to the soccer goalpost on the far side.

On the 1st and 2nd of November, we researched different design ideas and found one we liked. We also started making our website.

On the 3rd of November, we went to Bayly’s house and made our rocket. The first thing we did was measure out our rocket fuel. Our next step was to blend the clay kitty litter and cut a piece of PVC pipe to the right size. While holding the PVC pipe upright we poured the kitty litter in via a funnel. We then took the funnel out and while still holding the pipe we compacted the kitty litter using a stick (just smaller than the pipe) and a hammer. We then repeated the process using the rocket fuel we made earlier and then again with more kitty litter. We then left it and waited for it do harden up.

LAUNCH!

Nov 8, 2018

/Science Rockets

On the 6th of November, we drilled the hole through one end of the kitty litter and rocket fuel. We then made a wick and carved a cone-like top out of Styrofoam and hot glued that to the top of our rocket. We then placed the wick inside the hole that was drilled out of our rocket. At lunchtime, we then procceded to launch the rocket, but it didn’t have enough fuel to move. The rocket stayed stationary in the clamp while the fuel burned, all the smoke poured out of the rocket. For the rocket to work next time we would need to have added more fuel to the rocket body, as that was the reason the rocket did not launch.

WHAT WE CHANGED AND WHY + WHAT WE LEARNT/SCIENCE IDEAS

Nov 8, 2018

/Science Rockets

What we changed and why:

We changed the ratio of the of the potassium nitrate and icing sugar from 50%-50% to 65%-35%. We also changed the design of the rocket from made of paper to a PVC pipe the video we based our design off this video.

What we learned/science ideas:

We learnt that there is a chemical reaction between icing sugar and potassium nitrate when they are mixed together and ignited. We also learnt that there needs to be some sort of combustion to start the reaction and launch the rocket. Another we learnt was that you need to have the right ration of both icing sugar and potassium nitrate to make the rocket move.
